In [31]:
# Applying FOR-LOOP for calculating confusion matrix & Calculating matrix parameters.

for x,model in zip(model_names, models):
    
    model.fit(x_train,y_train)
    
    y_predict  = model.predict(x_test)
    
    
    confusion_matrix=metrics.confusion_matrix(y_test, y_predict, labels=[1, 0])
    cm = pd.DataFrame(confusion_matrix, index = [i for i in ["1","0"]],
                  columns = [i for i in [x+"_Predict 1",x+"_Predict 0"]])
    CM.append(cm)
           
    TP = cm.iloc[0,0]
    FP= cm.iloc[1,0]
    TN = cm.iloc[1,1]
    FN= cm.iloc[0,1]
    
    TPs.append(TP)
    FPs.append(FP)
    TNs.append(TN)
    FNs.append(FN)
    
    Recall.append(TP/(TP+FN))
    Specificity.append(TN/(TN+FP))
    Accuracy.append((TP+TN)/(TP+TN+FP+FN))
    Precision.append(TP/(TP+FP))
    Neg_Pred_value.append(TN/(TN+FN))

To interpret the models we need to first understand what is our requirement and accordingly what do we need from model.¶
We need to find the likelihood of a customer accepting the loan offer.¶
But likelihood of a customer accepting the loan offer depends on the customer's assessment of his ability to pay the EMI of the loan.¶
Hence, Customer's EMI paying capacity will determine his likelihood of accepting the loan offer.¶
It is fine as far as model predicts true values. We need to check to how much extent the model is predicting false values.¶

>>There two types of false values: False Positives & False Negatives

>>TPs -Acceptance of offer is predicted & customer accepts the offer -- means the customer has assessed that he has good emi paying capacity.

>>FPs - Acceptance of offer is predicted & customer does not accept the offer - means the customer has assessed that he does not have good EMI paying capacity.

>>FNs -No Acceptance of offer is predicted & customer has the capacity to pay the emi and wants emi. - Opportunity Loss

>>TNs - No Acceptance of offer is predicted & Customer also does not have the ability to pay the EMI and hence does not want the offer.

>> This means we need to minimise the False Positive values as Falsely giving the offer to a customer who does not have the EMI paying capacity may result in NPAs for bank.

  1. False Negatives are lost opportunities. The cost of lost opportunities is a small amount of interest charged on loan.
  2. False Postitives are predicting the customer will accept the offer and in reality cutomer has not accepted the offer. This happens when customer has assessed that he cannot pay the EMI and hence FALSE POSITIVES are heavy cost of extending offer to wrong people and Banks need to avoid the heavy cost to reduce the NPAs. This is because if a customer defaults entire loan amount is to be written off from the bank's profits.
  3. Hence there is a trade-off between forgone opportunies of small amount of interest charged on loan and heavy cost of NPAs.

Conclusion:- It is important to select a model which minimizes both on False Positives & False Negatives. But practically it may not be possible to minimize both together. We have to pick either FPs or FNs to minimize more.

Here, we will pick a model which minimizes False Positives. FP means:- Acceptance of offer is predicted by model and the customer does not accept the offer.

Note that ideally model should not predict likelihood of acceptance of offer by the customer when customer does not accept the offer. This non acceptance of offer by customer may be due to his assessment of his ability to not be able to pay the EMI

Hence False Positive(FP) is the error we need to reduce¶
